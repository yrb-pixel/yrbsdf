Four findings drive everything that follows.

One — the pain is linguistic, not technical. The most frequent word in verbatims is not "bug" or "data." It is some variation of clunky, not intuitive, hard to find, complicated. Data-quality and performance complaints exist, but they trail UX by a factor of three. When clients can articulate what they dislike, they are describing the feeling of using the workstation.

Two — the damage concentrates, and it is not random. Half of all Detractor NPS sit with the United Kingdom team. Within that pool, a handful of enterprise relationships generate the bulk of the volume: Raymond James alone accounts for 24 Detractor NPS, UBS for 16, abrdn and Lombard for 11 each, Citigroup for 9. These are not fringe accounts — they are some of the largest EMEA relationships.

Three — January's collapse follows a December we under-invested in. The mean score fell to 1.44 in January 2026, the worst month in the series. The preceding month carried the only material engagement dip: meetings per working day dropped 19% from the autumn average. Proactive touchpoints took the bigger hit — Client Entertainment fell 50% from its November peak, New User Training halved in December. By January, calls and "Dissatisfied Client" meetings were at their peak — the region was firefighting — but the score had already landed. The lagged correlation between engagement and the next month's NPS is moderate (+0.53), not deterministic, but the directional pattern is consistent across all three indicators.

Four — a meaningful share of Detractor NPS go unresolved. 30% of Detractor NPS have no resolution logged; a further 20% close with "client did not respond." Together that is exactly half the book where the voice-of-client signal never closes into a learning loop. The hours logged per case (median 15 minutes) raise a separate question about whether the follow-up motion is calibrated for the stakes involved.

A note on the data: this report analyses follow-up NPS cases generated by NPS responses of 0–6. It is by construction a detractor-only dataset — there are no passives or promoters here. Readings of "NPS performance" should not be drawn from this file. What it does tell us, with precision, is where EMEA is bleeding sentiment and why.

Part 01 · Scope & method

Reading this file for what it is.

The NPS follow-up case dataset is a mirror of unhappiness, not a measure of health. Calibrating the lens before drawing conclusions.

What the data contains

280 raw · 273 analysed

The file captures every detractor NPS generated in FY26 YTD in response to an NPS score of 0–6 across EMEA — the follow-up motion that consultants own when a detractor surfaces. It is comprehensive for detractors and blind to everyone else.

Exclusions applied for this analysis. Seven NPS were removed as confirmed user errors — clients who later told the consultant they had clicked the wrong number, or dismissed a pop-up by mistake, and who were in fact satisfied. Keeping them would have distorted the tail.

Part 04 · The December effect

What December cost us — and the coverage gap that costs us every month.

We now know what clients are saying and where. The remaining question is when the signal worsened, and what — if anything — the region could have done about it.

Engagement intensity vs. detractor scores

All 8 EMEA teams · Sep-25 → Apr-26

December was the only month where meetings per working day fell materially — 96.5 versus a Sep-Nov average of 128 (−27% from the November peak). January's mean NPS score then collapsed to 1.44. The lagged correlation between engagement intensity in month N and NPS score in month N+1 is +0.53.

The coverage gap

Engagement silence at detractor accounts

Across the 188 distinct detractor accounts in FY26 YTD — over eight months of data across all EMEA teams:

— 26% received no meetings at all.

— 21% received no connected calls at all.

— 14% (27 accounts) received neither a meeting nor a connected call — zero touchpoints in eight months, despite having logged at least one NPS score of 0–6.

This is the most actionable number in the report. A detractor account with zero engagement is a churn risk we are not managing.

Caveats on this read

Correlation, not causation. The +0.53 lagged correlation indicates a directional relationship but does not isolate cause. December's meeting dip may itself be a symptom of a quieter market (year-end, vendor budget cycles) rather than a lever the region controls independently.

Small monthly samples. The NPS panel ranges from 22 to 49 per month — statistically noisy. Readers should focus on the broad pattern, not on any single month's precision.

What we still cannot see. These datasets capture activity volume, not quality. A 30-minute troubleshooting call resolving a real pain point is not the same data point as a 5-minute drive-by.

Part 05 · Closing the loop

Once the signal arrives, what do we do with it?

A detractor NPS is the beginning of a conversation, not the end of one. The operational answer, at regional level, is that we are not closing that conversation reliably.

Resolution status of the 273 Detractor NPS

30% of Detractor NPS have no resolution text logged. A further 20% close with the client not responding. Exactly half the book never becomes a learning loop.

Open-case ageing

As of 15 Apr 2026

81 Detractor NPS are still open. Of those, 42 have been open for more than 60 days and 35 for more than 90 days. The oldest opened on 2 Sep 2025.

Open-case ageing on a detractor follow-up workflow is a leading indicator of churn exposure. A client who scored 0, was contacted late or not at all, and never heard a closure — that is an account that will not score 0 again; they will not respond at all next cycle.

Part 06 · Where to focus

Four moves that would change the number.

Not a remediation plan — a framing of where the highest-leverage work sits, so the operating teams can scope it with their own ownership.

Move 01 · Product · maps to Part 02

Treat the UX narrative as a strategic priority, not a support issue.

47% of written complaints are about navigation, search and intuitiveness. This is a brand-perception risk against Bloomberg, and it will not be resolved through case-level follow-up.

Move 02 · Account strategy · maps to Part 03

Escalate the five zero-mean enterprises to senior leadership.

Lazard, Canaccord, LGT, Nomura. Sixteen Detractor NPS between them, mean scores at or below 1.0. These relationships cannot be defended at consultant level.

Move 03 · Engagement floor · maps to Part 04

Close the 27-account coverage gap, then set a minimum engagement floor.

Twenty-seven detractor accounts received zero meetings and zero calls across eight months. That is a closeable gap in a CS ops sprint.

Move 04 · Operating rigor · maps to Part 05

Close the feedback loop — every case, every month.

A 30% no-resolution rate and 35 Detractor NPS open past 90 days point to an operating standard that needs tightening.
